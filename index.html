Tabletop RPG Tools

Helping GMs find tools to Level Up.

Running a tabletop roleplaying game is an amazing and rewarding social activity that can bring connection among the players, foster a love of reading, and many other attributes.

Getting a group together is complicated enough between locating players, deciding on a game and then game logistics (we have all fought the dreaded scheduling pit-fiend) that running the game itself should be fun and rewarding. Tracking information during the game, and also prepping for the session can be intimidating though there are some great tools to help you with your games.

Tools for Preparing and Running Games

Running and managing any TRPG game requires juggling different facets all at once including knowing the players, the rules, and adventure. Good game preparation can help make sessions manageable and avoid GM burnout. While paper and pencil are great it can help to have a digital tool that lets you search through your notes and also saves you some panicky moments if a dragon eats your notebook.

There is no perfect/right tool for game notes, and to borrow a phrase from the photography world, “The best note-taking app is the one you have with you.”1 so the key focus should be what application works best for YOUR specific needs (i.e. what works for one GM might be akin to fighting an owlbear for another).

I would also add an addendum to this idea to include, “The best note-taking app is one you have with you AND the one that is as complex as you need”.

Below is a list of different note apps that could be leveraged to create your own GM notebooks.
